The problem

Most failures are visible in hindsight.
Few organisations act in time.

Culture risk accumulates quietly. By the time issues surface publicly, the underlying conditions have been present for years.

Culture goes unexamined
Values are stated but not lived. Behaviours that undermine safety and inclusion go unchallenged because no structured mechanism exists to surface them.
Governance lacks teeth
Boards and leadership teams lack the tools to identify where accountability gaps exist in practice — not just on paper.
Risk is invisible until too late
The cost of unaddressed risk — staff turnover, regulatory intervention, safeguarding failures — is measurable. So is the cost of prevention, which is significantly lower.

The Culture & Governance Risk Diagnostic

A structured, independent engagement that gives your organisation a clear and honest picture of where culture and governance risk sits — and what to do about it.

What is the diagnostic?

A hands-on engagement, not a tick-box exercise.

The Culture and Governance Risk Diagnostic (CGRD) is a proprietary structured assessment covering three domains: Culture & Lived Experience, Risk & Forecasting, and Governance & Strategy.

Unlike pulse surveys or HR-led reviews — which are often extractive by design, with data flowing upward and solutions handed downward — the CGRD uses a coproduction methodology. The diagnostic is built with your employees, not simply conducted on them.

All interviews and focus groups are facilitated using trauma-informed practice, with clear informed consent, transparent data use, and active attention to participant wellbeing throughout.

How it works

Five phases. Eight weeks.

Designed to accommodate the working patterns of real organisations. Phases are sequential but can be compressed where the client has capacity and scheduling allows.

1
Discovery & scoping
Separate focus groups with SLT and staff. Scope agreed. Baseline survey launched.
2
Core diagnostic
One-to-one interviews and coproduction focus groups across role types and seniority.
3
Analysis
Thematic coding, sector benchmarking, and risk forecasting across all three domains.
4
Report & decision briefs
Full diagnostic report plus actionable decision briefs for your highest-identified risks.
5
Debrief workshop
Facilitated session with board and senior leadership — including defined staff representation.
What you receive

Board-ready outputs you can act on immediately.

Diagnostic Report
A comprehensive written report mapping culture and governance risks across all three domains, organised around cultural indicators — psychological safety, wellbeing, transparency, reporting confidence, values alignment — benchmarked against sector comparators.
Risk Map
A visual, at-a-glance summary of where risks sit across the three domains and their severity, including a forward-looking risk forecast with an inductive approach to identifying likely consequences if risks go unaddressed.
Decision Briefs
Short, focused documents (typically two to three pages) translating your highest-identified risks into plain-language evidence, coproduced solutions, and recommended next steps with indicative timelines. Designed to go directly into board and SLT discussions.
Leadership Debrief
A facilitated session to walk through findings, answer questions and align on priorities. Staff representation is included by design — the debrief is not a closed governance conversation.

Common questions

Frequently asked

How disruptive will this be to our organisation? +
The process is designed to be as light-touch as possible. We work around your schedule and can complete the diagnostic with minimal interruption to day-to-day operations. Phases are sequential but can be compressed where capacity and scheduling allow.
How confidential is the process? +
All findings are shared exclusively with the commissioning organisation. Interviews are conducted on a confidential basis and individual views are never attributed. The diagnostic operates under a named Data Ethics and Participant Protection Protocol, in full compliance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.
Can we commission just part of the diagnostic? +
Yes. We can scope a focused review of one domain, or tailor the engagement to a specific team, programme or governance question. All engagements are scoped individually — contact us to discuss your requirements.
How long until we receive the report? +
Typically 2–3 weeks after fieldwork is complete. For urgent commissions, an accelerated timeline can be made available.
What happens if a concern meets the threshold for safeguarding escalation? +
A clear escalation route is agreed with organisational leadership in the scoping phase and communicated to all participants in advance. All interviews are facilitated using trauma-informed practice with active attention to participant wellbeing.
Is the diagnostic right for our organisation right now? +
The diagnostic is most effective when leadership is genuinely open to hearing difficult findings and prepared to act on them. It is not appropriate as a standalone response to a live HR dispute, a safeguarding investigation, or an active regulatory enforcement process. We are transparent about these limits in every scoping conversation — which is free and without obligation.

Sectors we serve

The diagnostic is designed for any organisation that wants to lead with integrity — wherever it sits in the public, voluntary or social sector.

NHS Trusts & ICBs

Strengthening patient safety culture and board oversight

The NHS faces uniquely intense scrutiny on culture — from CQC inspection frameworks to the Ockenden and Francis inquiries, it is clear that patient safety depends on the conditions in which staff operate. The CGRD gives NHS Trusts and ICBs independent, board-level evidence of where culture and governance risk sits.

We understand the pressures of operating in complex, hierarchical systems where speaking up is difficult and culture change is slow. Our approach is designed to surface what formal structures miss.

Relevant for NHS organisations
  • ✓ CQC Well-Led domain preparation
  • ✓ Freedom to Speak Up environment assessment
  • ✓ Board-level culture risk visibility
  • ✓ Psychological safety across clinical teams
Relevant for charities
  • ✓ Charity Commission governance compliance
  • ✓ Trustee oversight of operational culture
  • ✓ Funder and commissioner assurance evidence
  • ✓ Staff retention in mission-critical roles
Charities & VCS organisations

Ensuring governance keeps pace with mission

Charities and voluntary sector organisations operate under constant resource pressure, with lean HR capacity and high reliance on staff motivation. When culture deteriorates, it deteriorates fast — and the cost in turnover, grievances, and funder confidence is significant.

UK boards are legally and regulatorily required to monitor organisational culture on an ongoing basis. The CGRD directly enables boards to meet this duty with evidence — not assumption.

Housing Associations

Accountability in complex, resident-facing organisations

Housing associations face growing regulatory scrutiny under the Regulator of Social Housing's consumer standards. Culture — particularly around how staff treat residents and how concerns are escalated — is increasingly central to regulatory assessment.

The CGRD supports housing associations in building the internal accountability structures and lived-experience evidence that regulators and investors increasingly expect to see.

Social Enterprises & Local Authorities

Setting healthy foundations for growth

Social enterprises growing rapidly often reach a point where informal culture norms — which worked at 10 people — begin to create risk at 30 or 40. The CGRD is particularly well-suited to organisations at this inflection point, before complexity makes culture change costly.

For local authorities, the diagnostic supports multi-team accountability reviews and provides evidence for elected members on where governance and culture risk sits within delivery structures.

Not sure if the diagnostic is right for your sector?

Every scoping conversation is free and without obligation. We will always tell you honestly if the diagnostic is not the right fit — and suggest what might be.
